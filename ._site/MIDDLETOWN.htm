UFO Sightings

        

July 12, 1996:
PRESS RELEASE

The research organization Tri-State Advocates for Scientific Knowledge has exhaustively investigated the reported Middletown UFO Disturbances that occurred on July 4, 5 and 6. On Thursday night, July 11 at 10:45 P.M., T.A.S.K. solved the mystery.

An eerie, self-luminous red object appeared and held in position at a high elevation shortly after 10:00 P.M. above the Middletown area, sending droves of befuddled residents milling into the streets in confusion and wonderment. Even traffic stalled in some quarters as the Middletown Police Department sought furiously to track the movement of the phantasmal specter amidst an unstoppable flurry of phone calls from kindly residents.

In a scene of great drama, T.A.S.K. investigator DALE FARMER struggled frantically through the streets and residential neighborhoods to pursue and document the strange event on videotape.

His unshakable determination led him to enact the foot pursuit of airplane that circled the object at close range. Upon chasing down the pilot of the plane which landed at Hook Airfield, Farmer was advised by the pilot that the object had been a red flare attached to and dangling from underneath two large helium balloons.

It is the contention of The Tri-State Advocates for Scientific Knowledge that the previous events in Middletown on July 3, 4 and 5 are as explainable as the event on July 11, which is obviously found to be a hoax. The determination that the previous sightings have also been hoaxed is derived from the description and behavior of the observed object in comparison with the explainable event of July 11.

Strangely, it must also be noted that during the July 3 through 8 time period, numerous UFO reports were generated from various parts of Ohio. These reports came in from Urbana, Ohio on July 3 and 8. A cone-shaped object was videotaped on Saturday, July 6 along the Ohio-Indiana border, south of the Paulding, Ohio area where a large crop-circle was discovered. The crop-circle was presumed by some to also be a man-made hoax, but the culprits have not come forward to claim responsibility.

Although the persons or organizations responsible for the deed in the Middletown area remains unknown, they should be advised that their actions could precipitate a potential fire hazard that could harm property or persons. In addition, a wide range of criminal charges could be rendered against these frauds, including inducing panic.

The Middletown Police Department, including Lieutenant Schwarber and Major Hoffman, should be highly recognized for their openness and professionalism in working with both T.A.S.K. and the news media during the unexplainable phase of the investigations.

The multiple hoaxes, taking place in the Ohio area (in conjunction with unsolved reports from other Ohio localities) suggests a series of events that may be larger in scope than previously thought.

What organization is capable of executing a complex hoax of such magnitude during the same time frame from diverse localities, and what is the purpose of the ruse? Is there a connection with the highly publicized motion picture Independence Day?

The T.A.S.K. organization, which holds that many UFO reports are unexplainable, will continue to track and investigate alleged UFO events in the Southern Ohio area with the singular objective of studying and explaining, when possible, the purported events for the purposes of scientific assessment.
